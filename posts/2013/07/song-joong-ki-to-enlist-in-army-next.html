  102. Black_PlagueAugust 1, 2013 at 9:49 AM

    Every job that requires you to work on a daily full-time basis is considered active duty, officially speaking - hence why you'll come across articles where celebrities who despite serving in the now-defunct celeb unit are labelled as 'going to serve active-duty' before actually going away.


    The real difference is the positions in active duty - and the majority of men in Korea tend to be put into combat/combat support units (like infantry, armor, artillery, combat engineers, air defense etc.). If not, then it's support units like field communications/signals, logistics, maintenance technicians and the like.


    Opposite of AD is reserve duty, who are only called up for training a few times a year and in case war break out, are expected to report for deployment.
