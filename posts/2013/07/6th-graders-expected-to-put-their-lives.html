    It means Primary School Leaving Exams. It is a national exam taken by all Primary Six (Grade 6) students in Singapore. The results of the exam will determine what kind of secondary school you will go to, how many years you have to study in secondary school and whether you have to take another national exam after the completion of your secondary education. It is getting increasingly more difficult every year and parents are spending all their money for their children to go to tuition classes in order to learn more. Hence, those who are not as financially well off are usually in disadvantage and have a higher chance of not able to succeed academically in the future. It hinders the social mobility of Singapore. For a twelve year old child in Singapore, it means you will have to study every single day and go for tuition classes almost everyday of the week after school and attend enrichment classes to differentiate you from your peers. It is highly stressful for a child at that age to handle. Also, it is unfair for a child to take a national exam at twelve year old which may affect their academic success as they may not be mature enough to understand the importance of it.
