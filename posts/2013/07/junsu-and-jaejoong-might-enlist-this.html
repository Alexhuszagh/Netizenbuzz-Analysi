  154. Black_PlagueJuly 19, 2013 at 4:55 AM

    Active duty upon enlistment is divided into several phases;

    1. Basic training.

    2. Occupation/position training (which can vary from infantry, engineers, technicians, armor, recon, maintenance, logistics, communications, artillery, medics etc.) + assigned to the unit you'll be staying in for the rest of your service term. The celeb unit was one such, until recently.

    3. General life as a soldier/marine/airman/sailor...and more training on the position you're in, which is intended on ensuring the troops know what to do with their assigned duties and various scenarios.

    Life overall can really vary. Troops in combat unit or combat support units obviously have it tougher + tend to be involved more in large-scale training exercises (such as Foal Eagle with US troops). Others have it more comfier - stereotypical belief among the public is that the Navy and Air Force folks have it really easy, in comparison to the Army and Marine Corps.

    Bluntly put, it highly depends what branch and what position you're in (and possibly, the unit as well - troops in elite prestigious divisions like the White Horse/Baekma supposedly train in the field far more often, as well as special forces)

    Cases of suicide, mental breakdowns and severe abuse/hazing up to the point of hospitalization also occur - particularly in the Army's front-line units and even more in the Marine Corps.
