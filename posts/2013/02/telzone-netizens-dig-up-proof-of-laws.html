[Telzone] Netizens dig up proof of 'Laws of the Jungle' being staged

Sunday, February 10, 2013  laws of the jungle, telzone  51 comments

Netizens somehow managed to track down all of the natives who appeared in 'Laws of the Jungle' and basically found that they were a part of a tourist organization that offered cultural experience tours through the area. 



^ is actually a 24 year old tour guide



On the show, this native below said that the 'Laws of the Jungle' cast/crew were the first outside contact they had with foreigners, but the following picture shows them with other foreigners.



-

Then there's an episode where the crew come across this river that they can't find a way around so they come up with a way for everyone to cross it. A quick search of the map shows that there are multiple routes around it. 




-

On another episode, the crew force themselves up a volcano using a difficult, unpaved path. Another EBS show had featured the same volcano, however, and it was revealed to be a tourist attraction with a paved road or the option to ride a car up. In total, the trip takes about 20 minutes. 




-


-

The next image is basically just a netizen claiming that the show made it seem like they slept overnight when really they just "posed" in their sleeping positions for 5 minutes before getting back up and claiming it was morning. The exact positions of their bodies, etc, are what give it away. 

-

This Telzone post (I won't be posting all the pictures because it's a lot) is basically saying that the show purposely dramatized a lot of the scenes to be more difficult than they are, because in reality, they're all tourist attractions that any regular person can climb up/walk through.






-

More proof that the show's "Siberia" episode was also another tourism course. The guy on the show lies saying that it's not a place anyone can go in and out as they please, but really you just need to pay for a ticket and you're good to go.

 -
