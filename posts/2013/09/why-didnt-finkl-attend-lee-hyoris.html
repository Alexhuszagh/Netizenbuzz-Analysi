    As time passed by, things changed. Human relationships are dynamic. I can assure you FinKL had friendship among them before. Definitely. It's impossible to say that they did not feel anything when they win any awards as a group or when they were singing together and seeing their fans cheering for them. At that instant, at that point of time, they probably were united and were together as one. However, when they drifted apart, everyone was doing their own stuff, and probably they contacted each other lesser and lesser, till the point when they were almost strangers.


    Friendships are easily shattered if they are not sustained properly. If the parties involve in the friendships do not make an effort to maintain it, then they will be strangers. People come and go.


    Of course, this may be due to their friendship wasn't that strong to start with. To be frank, they were co-workers. Each of them has their own social circles. Despite living together and performing together, they were not each other's best friend. They were just a group of people working together to earn money. There was no need for deep personal relationship. It might be even more awkward if you have a deep relationship with your co-workers because it might affect your work performance. If one of them (assuming they were best friends with each other at that point of time) quarreled with another, their emotions would show on national TV. It won't look nice. However, if they had a really plain relationship, they would not quarrel in the first place because there was nothing much to quarrel about. You can only quarrel with another person and get hurt by it if you are close with that person, because your emotions will get in the way. In this way, they could maintain a good and healthy working relationship.


    There are definitely exceptions, like SES and Shinhwa. In the case of Shinhwa, they quarreled and fought a lot (justified by Kangta in Kneedrop guru). They were close thus they could fight and quarrel. SES did not fight at all (according to past interviews). It could be true too.


    If you disagree with what I said, just think about all your relationship with your friends, current friends, childhood friends, best friends, acquaintances, your co-workers or classmates. Sometimes, you just can't contact each other anymore despite your strong urge to call them or even say 'Hi' on Facebook. You may be afraid of their cold responses and worried about what to say next. Things will become awkward. If you have performed before, you may understand what I said better. Sometimes, during the rehearsal for a performance for years, you may grow close to this group of people who are performing with you. You will share the joy when all of you receive praise for improving, and will share the tears when the rehearsal is downright horrible, and will share the anger if one of the performers is wrongly accused. Directly after the performance, you will feel that the world belongs to you and your group of performers. You will feel really happy and feel the strong friendship between all of you. However, once you stop the rehearsals, friendships start to drift apart, because there is nothing holding all of you together anymore.


    It is sad, but it's bits and parcels of life. Life moves on.
