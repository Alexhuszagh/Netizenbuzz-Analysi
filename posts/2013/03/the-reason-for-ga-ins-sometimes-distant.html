The reason for Ga-In's sometimes distant personality

Saturday, March 16, 2013  brown eyed girls, ga-in, kim eana  57 comments


Lyricist Kim Eana, in a 2010 interview:

"When Ga-In was younger, she grew up with a lot of pain that developed into wounds. She has a habit of facing the wall whenever she cries, and for a while, all we ever saw was her back. She eventually just turned those cries into silence, and I think that's why people get the impression that she's arrogant..."

-

"She's never one to complain or whine. She's grown far too much since then... Actually, I don't think grown is the appropriate word because she learned to hide herself instead."

-

After mentioning that fandoms of another idol group would swear at her, throw objects, and point their fingers at her at broadcast halls or event performances:

"Sometimes, I grew so enraged seeing that that I just wanted to give up my position as staff and grab those kids by the neck collar to teach them a lesson. Ever since then, Ga-In has had trouble opening herself up to anyone she isn't close with and treating them with sincerity."

-

Kim Eana composed and wrote a song for Ga-In titled 'Ga-In'

Whenever I turned around and acted like I didn't know
I heard their whispers
and when I smiled at them like nothing was wrong
They looked down at me again
Believe only what you see, take only what you're given
Just don't come any closer than this
Like a bird who's never flown, I hide my wings
Like I've never shed a tear, I hide myself
Like I've never been hurt, I hold it in and smile

-
