    actually the percentage given to digital sales will only give you an edge if you are an absolute beast on the digital charts. anyone with strong physical sales will have the same advantage.


    it works like this:
    say 20% digital sales, 5% is physical sales.


    the music show calculates based on what share you take of each. boy groups who sell a lot of albums grab nearly all 5% of the pie because physical album sales are so poor (so super junior selling 100k albums a week means they're selling >90% of all physical albums, so they'll get >90% of that 5% share.).


    digital sales are a lot more equitable. even though digital sales are 20% of the calculation, I doubt most "digital monsters" manage to make up even 1/4 of ALL digital sales sold that week, so they'd be getting less than 5% of that 20%.


    obviously for artists who are beasts at digital sales, it's possible for them eat up a larger share of digital sales and earn a larger part of the 20%.


    Hope that makes sense. Sorry if it's confusing.


    tl;dr fangirls shouldn't complain that music charts are biased toward digital sales over physical sales because the two markets have VERY different distributions.
