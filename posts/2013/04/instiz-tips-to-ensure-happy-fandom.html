[Instiz] Tips to ensure a happy fandom career

Saturday, April 06, 2013  instiz, LOL  154 comments


1. Star is talented = If the star is talented, there's a less of a chance that the star will be hated on by netizens or other fandoms so there's no need to shield them.

2. Star care of their private lives = Less of a chance that they'll get onto the chopping block and into scandals and controversies, which means less of a need for shields.

3. Star isn't a hate it or love it star = If the star has a clear divide in that you either hate him or love him, you have to deal with both hardcore psycho fans and extremely hateful antis. More experienced fans will know that it's difficult to deal with fandoms of top celebrities because smaller fandoms means less antis which means a comfortable fan life.

4. Star isn't an idol = This is optional but stanning an idol makes for a more difficult fan life because of fandom competition. If you want a comfortable fan life, go for a singer or an actor. Idols also make more money than other professions so companies are prone to release nonstop merchandise and albums. Stanning an idol means having an empty wallet.

5. Star doesn't use an SNS account = Twitter and other SNS make up the majority of celebrity scandals and chopping blocks. Whenever a scandal happens, Twitter is usually the first to attack. It's safer to just not have an account at all.

6. Star is faithful to his profession = Star stays away from overexposing themselves at events and through CFs and sticks to singing if he's a singer and acting if he's an actor. The public is more trusting of a star who sticks to their job.

7. Star has short hiatuses = It's difficult as a fan to stan a star who's gone for long periods of time.

8. Star is honest = This doesn't mean that the star has to share everything about their private life, but showing a certain level of honesty is always good. Stars who are too mysterious with their private lives have a higher chance of driving their careers into the ground once somthing goes wrong in the future.

9. Star that the public likes = Also optional, but it's always easier as a fan to stan a star who gets popular and does well on his own without necessarily the help of a fandom.
