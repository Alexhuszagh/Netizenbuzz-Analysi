    It's actually my favorite drama of all time, so I'm a bit biased here when I say I adore it. If you like TW dramas, I think there's a fair chance you may like it (although I guess everyone's taste is different).


    The Taiwanese version is wonderful. Like the Korean version, Ariel Lin and Bolin Chen play best friends. The series begins at Cheng You Qing (Ariel)'s 30th birthday, so the TW leads are younger than the Korean leads. The high school portions are also far less exaggerated and more believable. The series deals with the two leads's romantic lives, both with other people and eventually, with each other. You also see their family lives (Da Ren's mom and sister, You Qing's two siblings + parents), their other love interests and exes.


    The series is beautifully directed, with lots of quiet reflective moments--one of my favorite recurring visuals is the use of Ariel's reflection in a mirror talking back to her when she's conflicted. Just a lovely way of showing inner dialogues. Or characters appearing together in the same frame even when you know they're apart, just to show they're on the phone or thinking of each other. The series is punctuated with lots of heartfelt moments and thoughts about love (romantic, platonic, family). Bolin and Ariel have wonderful chemistry together--believable as friends and lovers. Overall, the series feels so natural. Not too much shouting/crazy plot twists/insane drama. It's so watchable, yet earnest and sweet. Really different from other TW dramas I've seen and Korean ones as well.
