  48. TaehyunJuly 23, 2015 at 3:22 AM

    Really goes to show how important image is in the mainstream music industry, especially in Korea.
    Since people care more about relating to, looking up to, or connecting with an artist than their music.
    Cheetah cut her hair and added some make up and then suddenly had people calling her ''fierce'' ''queen'' etc, for it.
    But it's obviously not like she's suddenly a different person, nor did it change her skill level, it's an image that struck since people liked it and made it bigger than her actual music etc (people often run with these images when it ''speaks'' to them, making it no longer about music).
    She's obviously been making music for years before cutting her hair and adding some make up, but nobody really cared.
    She even came out on SMTM before that, but nobody really cared and she pretty much just was a background character, till the makeover (which didn't affect her skill of course), it fit the show and a lot of the girl fans, so it made her bigger than her artistry.
    Coma was pretty bad from a musical standpoint, but it did just what it had to do to keep the image alive etc, realistically an album actually written by her would be pretty bad, which is evident from her songs before her makeover, the image is bigger than the artistry, and a sudden popularity boost because of something outside of music, doesn't alter her actual skill, she's nowhere near ready for an album, even the Coma song was JUST on par..never mind the fact that her competition was mostly complete garbage (puss puss etc).


    Same thing with Jessi, she's a terrible rapper (obviously Cheetah isn't as bad) and doesn't really know how to make songs, but just because she went on the show and acted brash and basically acted ''Western'' which was a new thing to see on a Korean show, at the time, she gained some fame and fans.
    It makes sense from a technical standpoint, Jessi with her attitude, attracted international fans for seeing something relatable and ''new'' on a Korean show + Korean girls etc that looked up to someone doing what they couldn't/maybe even wanted to.
    I love the fact that people use both of these cases to put other actual artists down, just because they think the image is ''fierce'' or ''a real female rolemodel'', just because one cut her hair and added make up, an added image.
    As if that's anything notable.
    Her music is still the same, it barely meets the quota, that one song was just enough to supplement the image, a whole album, even with the image, won't be anything great.
    Yes, people do care less about the actual music when the image is more prominent, but that's to an extent, plus it doesn't change the music quality.
    Popular groups/artists, for example EXO, get an image and a lot of fans and then have songs that meet the quota, written by actual writers etc, just to sustain it.
    If groups like that made entire albums by themselves, they'd crumble.
    Now take Cheetah, her image and hype is there, but way smaller, but her skill doesn't even match that (or she'd be famous for her music before it happened for the make up/haircut etc), so having her make an entire album by herself, would be completely underwhelming, since it'd pretty much be the same old artist, who didn't gain any fame before, making an album, but now with an added ''hyped up'' image.
    They could also go completely left field and decide to have others write/make her album, just because her image is too valuable with all the girl fans she has now etc.
