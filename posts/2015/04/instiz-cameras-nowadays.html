    Yes. Fansite masters tend to invest $1000's of dollars into their gear. Many of the well known names in larger fandoms always appear to be toting the newest, biggest, baddest camera on the market. It's a clever system if you think about it: Fansites take amazingly HQ photos & videos which they then painstakingly edit until every shot/frame is as good as its going to get, slap a watermark on and upload galleries of their labor of love online. Fellow fans appreciate the quality and charm and are in turn incentivized to purchase professional-grade photobooks, DVDs and various other goods that boast even higher quality captures sans the watermarking.


    So while the fansite masters may go bankrupt affording their equipment initially, they make it all back (and then some) in profits. The last photobook/goods combo I purchased put me out $50 (and that is NOT including shipping to the US /cringe), so if you imagine that fansite sold 200 copies (which some of the bigger ones do easily), you realize those camera savvy unnies are making bank. In the case of most fansites I've encountered, a significant portion of the profits from goods sales goes into an account for their bias for birthdays and support, another portion may be donated to a charity in the fandom's name, and the final portion goes into the master's pocket to cover fangirling expenses and equipment repairs/upgrades. Like I said, clever system.


    But if you have a talent for capturing an idol's best angles and moments, and you have no qualms about traveling often and huge crowds, it's a sound investment in my opinion. And it looks totally fun! There are many accounts of fansite masters being on friendly terms with their idol(s), to the point of receiving unique attention (see Must Romantic/Jonghyun, Shining Smile/Fany, Flying Petals/Taeyeon, uhhh...there are a lot). Some fans even ship certain masters with their idol! Idols know they can trust OOO to make them look good, and in turn OOO earns a special place in their idol's heart and mind. Everybody wins.


    ** I referred to the fansite owners as female here (most are girls), but there are a handful of top quality sites run by fanboys too, such as Busan Boy/Daehyun & Suave Island/Kyungsoo.
