    As someone who works in the wastewater and drinking water process, I will say that, even though we will not run out of potable water "soon" it is definitely something that is a huge concern. All of the water is being used in one form or another and even then there amount of water going through Niagara falls isn't as much as it has been in the past. This water is eventually taken into drinking water plants where they have to treat it to make it drinkable for the human population free of possible germs/bacteria/or pathogenic organisms.

    There is also the problem with people being unwilling for treated wastewater (the water that people have essentially bathed in, used for the toilet, etc etc) to be directly used for drinking water due to that "ew" factor when you think that you're drinking essentially "pee" water. What people don't realize is that this water has been treated so all of that waste has been treated and the water is perfectly fine. And if you think about it you're also drinking dinosaur waste too but nobody cries out about this. This leads them to dumping this treated water that can be treated for drinking water right away into the ocean/rivers which lead to the ocean where it is mixed with salt water. This makes treating water 10 times as difficult to treat as well as costly.

    So essentially yes we have a problem. Some countries have too much rain/water which is just as bad as the parts where there isn't enough water. And don't state that they can just truck it around. It isn't simply that easy. So yes, they take facts to another level but the best action is always prevention. You don't start caring when the problem is too far for you to fix.
