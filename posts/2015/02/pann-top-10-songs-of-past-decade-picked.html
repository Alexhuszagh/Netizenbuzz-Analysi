Saturday, February 21, 2015  pann  159 comments

Pann: Media outlet picks the TOP 10 hit tracks out of the past 10 years

(In order of release)


1. Lee Seung Gi's "Because You're My Woman" (2004)
The #1 karaoke song for older woman/younger man couples. This song shot Lee Seung Gi to top star fame in just his debut.


2. Jang Yoon Jung's "Omona" (2004)
The song took off thanks to Jang Yoon Jung's unique vocal characteristics. It was also the first time in 12 years that a trot song won #1 on a public broadcast music show since Kim Soo Hee in 1993.


3. SG Wannabe's "To Live" (2005)
The group is known for having the most hit tracks in the digital music market. The album sold 400,000 copies and went on to win Daesang at the 'Golden Disk' that year.


4. Baek Jiyoung's "I Won't Love" (2006)
Successfully pulled off her transformation from a dance singer to a ballad singer with the mega hit of this song. For the first time in six years, she won #1 on public broadcast and was also named the #1 favorite karaoke song of the year.


5. Big Bang's "Lies" (2007)
The group's representative song as one of the industry's best male groups. It was also chosen by 'M! Countdown' as the song that stood out the most in the past decade.


6. Wonder Girls' "Tell Me" (2007)
The legendary 'Tell Me' syndrome brought on parodies from soldiers, police, teachers, and every walk of life in society. Put the entire nation in retro fever.


7. SNSD's "Gee" (2009)
The song that put wings on SNSD. Won #1 for 9 weeks in a row on the Mnet chart and was at the top for 7 consecutive weeks. Also broke records set by Baek Jiyoung's "Like Being Hit by a Bullet" and Big Bang's "Haru Haru".


8. IU's "Good Day" (2010)
The song that propelled IU to become the nation's little sister. The 'three high notes' still remain as her trademark.


9. Psy's "Gangnam Style" (2012)
The song that not only shook up Korea but the entire world. Stood at #2 on the Billboard singles chart for 7 consecutive weeks and took #1 on the UK singles chart. The music video also recorded the most views on YouTube.


10. Busker Busker's "Cherry Blossom Ending" (2012)
Took the entire nation by storm with their cherry blossom-themed song that manages to climb back into the charts every spring. It is now an unforgettable 'seasonal' song.
