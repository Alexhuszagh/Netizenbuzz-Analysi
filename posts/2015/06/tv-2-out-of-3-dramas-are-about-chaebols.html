  61. chilsungieJune 15, 2015 at 8:19 PM

    Whoop. UK dramas getting a mention. Haha.

    I think the romance isn't the issue in Korean dramas. It's that it becomes such a huge genre aspect, that it either needs another huge genre aspect to join with it, for example an action-romance like 'Healer' was amazing because the two genres are vital to each other and make the whole drama that much better. The romance genre is really simple, however to make something with high viewer ratings, that secondary genre needs to be strong. That's why for romance-heavy dramas, it's harder for them to get good ratings just based on plot alone. So off the top of my head, great dramas with kind of 50/50 romance + strong other genre, are things like Healer, Rooftop Prince, King 2 Hearts and That Winter. Then if it is romance-heavy and doesn't have another strong secondary genre, it has to rely on smart script-writing, so I'm thinking Reply 1997, Personal Taste, The Greatest Love, which make you squirm at the romantic-ness of it all and lean on comedy or makjang elements. These, I think, are much harder to get big, but they are the easiest to write and when they are successful. They get huge. Looking at you BOF, You're Beautiful and Winter Sonata.


    It doesn't mean that you need romance to make a great drama, however. You just see it less and less. Like Heartless City, which was constantly talked about, lacked romance, but was so fun to watch worked.

    So yeah, just what I think.
