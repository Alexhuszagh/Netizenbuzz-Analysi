  48. QQBBQMarch 3, 2015 at 3:39 PM

    There are practical and there are cultural/sociological reasons.

    First is that English fluency and even the *acculturation* of understanding foreign cultures is a key asset for many aspects of Korean life. Not only getting jobs but things like marriage and even SOCIAL standing.

    Speaking English (and other foreign languages to a lesser degree) is seen as not only a technical skill, but a sort of shorthand for cultural elitism. This goes back to Japanese Colonial Period and then the American War and Occupation, where people with fluency were the few Koreans who had been elite and intelligent enough to have been educated abroad or have family/social/economic ties enough so they interacted with Americans on an equal footing.

    It's like "knowing which fork to use" was a shorthand for being upper class in English dominated societies (forks themselves being a top-down imposition in British society in the Middle Ages). Or black Americans after the late 1800s adopting vaguely French sounding names to affect the educated Haitan and other mulatto migrants who had been educated and acculturated by the French to equal or superior level of white American elites.

    Second is a weird specific thing to Korea, where Koreans are fixated with the larger world. Koreans have *ALWAYS* been extremely outwardly focused and obsessed with foreign ideas. Korea is an extremely conservative and in some ways assbackwards society but they are intellectually incredibly open to new foreign ideas. . They have been... for at least 1000 years... fixated on sending their kids to the dominant power and learning from them and bringing it back to Korea. During Yi the Korean elites sent generations of their best kids to Qing. During Meiji they sent their kids to Japan. Today, America is best so they sacrifice the entire family to send their kids to the US.

    In a way, because even average kids and kids from shitty families can study abroad, it's irresistible to many Koreans because *they* (being a storeowner or whatever) can do what GENERATIONS of their farmer forefathers dreamed of doing: sending their crappy kid overseas to the Great Power to become educated. It's something that only nobles and the best of the best could do throughout Korean history, but anyone can do now... since American colleges are so greedy for the premium they can charge students from Asia.

    The irony is that colleges cut off financial aid and set up racist quotas to keep out Asian American kids who actually like work and die here in the US but they set up these super expensive programs for Asians from Asia to finance their white and black students.
