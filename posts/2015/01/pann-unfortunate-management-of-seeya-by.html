[Pann] The unfortunate management of SeeYa by CCM

Sunday, January 11, 2015  ccm, kim kwang soo, nam gyuri, pann, seeya  267 comments



Girl group SeeYa made their debut in 2006 with the album 'Woman's Scent'. 
They didn't start off the bat with having a 'focal member', and the group was generally known for having the standard 'pretty member' and 'member who sings well'. 


But all of a sudden, members Lee Boram and Kim Yunji became second to new 'focal member' Nam Gyuri. As you can see, they're barely visible in their promo picture above. 


The bias doesn't end there, as Nam Gyuri would be dressed in differentiating outfits from the other members all the time, such as in skirts/dresses and darker make up.


These outfits garnered their company a lot of hate, as it was in the winter and many wondered why only Nam Gyuri was dressed in a fur jacket and the other members were left to dry. 


The most controversial of all.. an event promo poster describing the group as "Nam Gyuri and SeeYa"


Once again, she's the only one in the skirt. 


Accusations of bias deepened during their 'Shall We Get Married' promos when only Nam Gyuri, from start of the promos to the end, was dressed in a bridal headdress. 





Other stages




That's member Lee Boram in the back, not a back dancer^



Their controversial album cover with Nam Gyuri's picture as the largest frame




'Gani' promos where, once again, Nam Gyuri was the only one given a special focal point to her outfit with a butterfly hair pin. 



Lee Boram doesn't even have a bracelet^


The picture above was from a year-end ceremony stage that got CCM a lot of hate. The focus was entirely on Nam Gyuri the entire time and viewers basically had no idea that Supernova or the rest of SeeYa was even there performing with her because they looked like her back dancers. 

-

Nam Gyuri eventually left the group, leaving a post on her mini-homepage and fancafe talking about discord. In a press conference, Lee Boram and Kim Yunji tried to retort her claims by dissing her back but to the public/media, all they saw was a female celebrity up against a large company and sympathy went to Nam Gyuri. 


Lee Boram and Kim Yunji stayed in CCM and went on to release two more albums as Women's Generation and Wonder Woman but only as collaboration projects with labelmates. The projects were basically to promote Jiyeon. 


They then had another album with Co-Ed's Soomi before her debut with Co-Ed for 'Rebloom', but that, too was just another promotional tactic for Soomi. 


SeeYa eventually decides to disband and decide to get together for one last album with Nam Gyuri. As always, she's back in the center. 


Still the center


And their final broadcast together

-

What the three are up to now:

Nam Gyuri: People expected her to be buried by Kwang Soo but she's been doing well. Released two singles, participated in a variety of drama and movie OSTs, acted in dramas and movies, judged on 'Superstar K', reunited with Kwang Soo. Has tons of pictorials since she's pretty, models for cosmetics brands, etc. Doing quite well for herself.

Lee Boram: Renewed her contract with Mnet after SeeYa's disbandment. No singles, some participation in drama OSTs. Was in the musical 'Falling For Eve'. Released an album with Superstar K's Lee Boram called '2Boram' but it didn't do well. Was also embroiled in a scandal when her ex-boyfriend commit suicide (Chae Dong Ha) and people blamed her because it somehow got out to the media that she ignored a text he sent. Apparently, Chae Dong Ha was reminded of her and sent her a text before his death and Lee Boram, not wanting to rekindle a relationship with an ex again, ignored it (not fully knowing of his suicidal situation either). 

Kim Yunji: Did not renew her contract with Mnet. She was the best singer in the group but only released one single since the disbandment without any promotions. Has not participated in any drama OSTs. Lives life as a normal 'non-celebrity' now and attends school. She sang at her school festival but nobody knew her as a celebrity, just as a fellow classmate.

In conclusion: This is what happens when you focus all your resources on one member of a group.
