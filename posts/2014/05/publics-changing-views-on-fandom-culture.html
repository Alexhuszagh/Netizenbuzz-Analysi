Public's changing views on fandom culture

Sunday, May 11, 2014    136 comments


Article: From 'fangirl' to 'Star Baragi'... fandoms come to the TV

Source: Sports Korea via Naver

Article talks about the growth of fandoms over the years, like how they've moved away from crazy attention-seeking antics and expensive fan gifts to charity/volunteer work, donating to causes, etc.

Also talks about the new TV show 'Star Baragi' where longtime fans of stars offer their point of views.

PD said, "Fandoms are a culture that every generation, from children to adults, can connect with."

"There are a lot of new hobbies that didn't exist before. Fans have so many ways to be happy. If your life is happy because of a star, then it's something meaningful. There are a lot of extremes with fandoms but there are also positive aspects. In the past, being a fan wasn't something to be proud of it but recently, people have been showing it off more."
