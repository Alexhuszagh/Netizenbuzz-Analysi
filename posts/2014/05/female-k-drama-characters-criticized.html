Female k-drama characters criticized for lack of development

Thursday, May 29, 2014  korean dramas, tv  581 comments



Article: Female characters fall behind fast moving dramas

Source: Ize via Naver

Article talks about the lack of development in female characters compared to the variety in genres and interests/professions of the male characters.

New Leaf: Park Min Young's character is the cliche 'fate-like' romance where she chances upon her love interests while the other male characters have other interests and obligations outside of romance like their work in the court. She spends her time being falling in love and being jealous over 'Kim Suk Joo'.

You're All Surrounded:  Lee Seung Gi is busy working with his investigation team as well as investigating his mother's murder while Go Ara is involved in, like always, romance related activities like randomly kissing the bare chest of Eun Dae Goo, not having the credentials for her job, and putting her passion before her actual skills.

Doctor Stranger: Lee Jong Suk is a prodigious doctor and shows himself being a professional in his field while his lover Jin Se Yeon remains as his love interest and nothing more.

Kapdong: Kim Min Jung falls in love with prosecutor Yoon Sang Hyun.
