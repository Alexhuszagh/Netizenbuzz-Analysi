    Note: My post is rather lengthly, but I believe it is an unbiased piece.
    you can ignore my username for this post because even though I am a SONE,
    I listen to and observe almost all aspects of the Korean entertainment industry.


    Back in July 2012, I went on a 6 month company attachment in Korea (You will be surprised how badly the IT industry in Korea needs English translators / technical personnel)


    Throughout the attachment, I didn't really have much work to do and thus I went around, absorbing the culture and talking to anyone and everyone who was willing to talk.


    From locals at bus stops, tourists, students, ahjumma shopkeepers, anyone.


    Bulk of the time I was in Seoul, but I went on short trips to Boryeong (Mud Festival), Gyeongju (scenery) and Jeonju (food and Taeyeon shrine :>)


    I stayed in Guesthouses (because that's where Korean backpackers stay) while on day trips and yes and as many have found out: idol groups are not everyone's cup of tea.
    It's like the pop scene in any other country: main demographics consists of teenagers, young adults.


    Here is a pretty good article (albeit a little old) about who's really popular in Korea
    http://www.asianjunkie.com/2014/02/k-pop-eh-heres-the-11-musical-artists-that-everyday-koreans-actually-listen-to/


    Missing is:
    Brown-Eyed Soul
    Whee Sung
    B2ST (crazily popular for an idol)
    Akdong Musician (debut recently)


    And well, being a SONE, after introductions, I will go straight for the jugular and ask "who is your favourite girl group" or "what girl groups do you know?"

    Questions along those lines.


    Unsurprisingly, SNSD is a practically a household name.
    They may not be a fan of the group, some didn't even like the group but they all know who SNSD is.
    The Nation's Girl Group title is not just for show.


    Moving on, most of the
    locals I had the chance to interrogate (sit down and have a proper conversation)


    actually had trouble naming a 2nd girl group (this was back in 2012 when groups like A Pink were still relatively new and Girl's Day still building up their fanbase)


    Majority of them can't even name f(x), who is also from SM Entertainment. lol


    The most common 2nd Girl Group named was 2NE1, a few interviewees referred to them as Big Bang's dongsaeng group or little sisters.


    I have it written somewhere (number of mentions, comments about the various groups),
    but okay, to cut the long story,
    from my findings (I "interviewed" easily a few hundred people, I even talked to my colleagues' entire families and their grandparents with my broken Korean)


    Sistar is a group that does not come to mind easily (this was even before Frozen came out, which Hyorin did a fantastic cover of).


    Yes, they do go on variety shows.

    Yes, they do have CF deals (I distinctly remember seeing as this mini 4-figure sticker everyday for months on the train windows)


    But they are not on TV all the time (no idol group is).
    So yea, Koreans actually have trouble naming the group, it just doesn't come easily to them.


    Even with the aid of many bottles of Soju (which is like practically water to them).


    And even with me asking specifically about girl groups.


    Of course, this is the findings of ONE guy (me).
    And to start with, majority of the locals can't even name 3 idol groups consecutively, let alone 2 girl groups.

    I was in Korea for 6 months,
