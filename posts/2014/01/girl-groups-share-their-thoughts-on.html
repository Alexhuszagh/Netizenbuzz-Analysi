Thursday, January 09, 2014    229 comments


Article: 'Sexy battle' Girl groups open up on the controversy surrounding sexy concepts

Source: Newsen via Nate

Girl's Day: "There is a move where we caress our legs with a feather. Honestly, we like being told we're sexy. We (put in moves like this) to look sexy." Girl's Day CEO: "We've been thinking of a concept like this since a year ago. Since it can't be just sexy, it was difficult to draw a suitable line with their choreography and outfits."

Rainbow rep: "The concept is voyeurism. A bit fantasy-like, it leaves sexy thoughts to the imagination, which is why we went with a polaroid type film set. Our plan was to make imaginations possible instead of showing skin outright."

Dal Shabet: "Our sexiness is a mature but charismatic sexy. Our outfits don't have skin exposure so we had to emphasize our sexiness in our choreography."

Girl's Day's Minah: "Regarding sexually suggestive controversies, we're trying to express art so we don't want people to view it in that way."
