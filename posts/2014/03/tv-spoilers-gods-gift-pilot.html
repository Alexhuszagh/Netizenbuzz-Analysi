  72. ArielLM13March 4, 2014 at 5:28 AM

    I understand that but honestly the acting industry and the music industry are both apart of one industry. The entertainment industry. Truthfully speaking, the industry becomes less saturated when people cross over because it means there is less of a need to find new people.


    For example, let's say we have 200 individual idols of relativity known status. Now let's say we also have some 75 actors of relatively known status. The number for actors is lower because actors get repeatedly cast once they gain a certain amount of public recognition. Even if some act bad, they will be cast because people will come to see them. However, you(a director) can only use the same actors so many times. You need new people to fill in the roles because complacency loses attention which, in turn, loses money. You try rookies but they come with a price. You are not guaranteed a good turn out of people willing to watch a rookie and you are not guaranteed that people will like the rookie. You lose money but the rookie stays in the industry. They can get by for a while by saying "so and so" cast me until they strike gold or land into obscurity. You, still, need more new faces but you don't want to take the chance on a rookie again unless they blow you away with talent, which doesn't happen that often and still has most of the risk. Then it hits you, an idol. Someone who you know can get you the most important thing, money. Even If they end up being bad, you don't have to lose money for it. A rookie with close to none of the risk.
