    Physical albums sold only show you how big a fandom is. It rarely ever equates to overall popularity. And since a lot of groups hold events, where buying an album is required for a chance to get up close and personal with your bias, it means that fans are buying more than one album at a time. There's also the case of groups releasing multiple versions of an album, which usually comes with different photocards, and hardcore fans will buy up as many as they can.

    In the end, both have their benefits, but when it comes to popularity with the masses, the best indicator is always digital sales.
