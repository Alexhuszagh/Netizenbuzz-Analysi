    depends on the vowels

    when you say 'oh' tongue goes back and jaw moves with that
    when you say 'eh' goes forwards


    In korean 'oh' 'eo' 'oo' 'eu' are said in the back of the throat, 'ah' is said down, 'ae' 'ee' 'eh' 'ui' are said forewards


    the direffence between 'eo' and 'oh' is the height, eo is said with a more open mouth while 'oh' is so closed it can be confused with 'oo' sometimes


    the difference between 'oo' and 'eu' is the lips spreading, you say 'oo' with round lips but 'eu' with spread lips


    there are many ways a jaw can move to make vowels sound like theyre supposed to


    if you try to pronounce 'ee' with open mouth, meaning the distance between upper and lower teeth is big, you will end up saying something like 'eh'
