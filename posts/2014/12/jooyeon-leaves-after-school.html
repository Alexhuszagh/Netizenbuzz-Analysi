  147. Persephone BasilissaDecember 21, 2014 at 9:53 PM

    As a fan of Pledis groups, I've been watching the company pretty closely over the last couple of years. Even though After School has been active in Japan and various members have had non-music projects, the loss of Kahi was key. She had a particular charisma that led the girls forward with purpose, as well as a special relationship with Pledis Boss. Jungah has probably had a much harder time advocating for more Korean comebacks, I think, than Kahi would have had.

    Also, with Nu'est comebacks being conceptually and geographically all over the map and with Ara & Yoonjo being pulled from Hello Venus, my sense is that Boss & staff are still learning how to manage more than 1 or 2 groups -- especially the part about developing, cementing and maintaining a group's identity.

    My impression is that since 2012, when Boss announced his plans for a 17-member boy group, the company has been pretty focused on that. From what I've seen about various trainees being recruited, trained & eliminated, it seems the project is much more time- and labor-intensive than Boss anticipated.

    Though they've been taking too damn long to pull the group together, they're developing the boys more deeply than most companies do before debut. They not only perform difficult choreography extremely well, but they also routinely design their own choreo. The rappers are not the only ones writing their own lines; several of the members also write entire songs. When recording songs, one member directs and produces the others. And the boys have been performing concerts and guest stages for over 2 years now, including being their own MCs.

    From what I've seen of Seventeen's recent performances, I'd say Pledis is still experimenting with their identity and image, though. They've got 3 aspects being developed right now: hip hop (a l Brand New Music), ballads (especially acoustic ones with the boys playing their own guitars), and R&B-inflected dance pop along the lines of TVXQ!, Michael Jackson and Justin Timberlake. The stage outfits from this weekend's performance incorporated all 3 identities -- some members styled one way and some another.

    Pledis will have to pull it together somehow, make their public image one thing. Once they achieve that -- which will hopefully be at debut (sometime in the next few months?) -- then Seventeen can to some extent manage themselves and Pledis Boss & staff can return their attention to their other talent.

    I believe the company's core duty for these other groups will be the same as it currently is for Seventeen: find each group's core identity and proceed with regularly-scheduled releases that cement and elaborate on that identity. (For an example of a company doing this well, refer to Jellyfish Entertainment and VIXX.) Pledis will need to focus their efforts in a clear-minded, deliberate fashion on After School, Nu'est, Orange Caramel, Seventeen, Dambi, Kahi and Donggeun.

    Along these lines, I believe it's entirely possible that Ara & Yoonjo may either join AS or be the core of a new girl group. Pledis Girls' dance skills are up to par already. It will really be a matter of making sure their vocals are solid, choosing a viable concept and having the resources to promote it well.

    That said, Pledis has done so very right by Orange Caramel. They can at least manage one group well, with lots of different concepts inside a cohesive identity. They can promote them creatively and successfully. And though juggling all of these elements for this many acts can be tricky as hell, it should be doable if Pledis Boss & staff just focus their attention and make smart decisions.
